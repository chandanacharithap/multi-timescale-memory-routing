\section{Bundled Freshness: The Positive Six-Action Result Still Matters}

We keep the bundled \texttt{freshness\_v1} result as the controlled updated-knowledge experiment from the preprint. That benchmark still gives the cleanest positive six-action result in the project. The full router reaches `0.867` answer quality and `0.133` stale-answer rate, outperforming retrieval-only and memory-only while using meaningful \texttt{FAST\_ADAPT}, rare guarded \texttt{CONSOLIDATE}, and explicit rollback.

We retain this result in the journal draft for two reasons. First, it is the only benchmark in the repo that currently exercises all six actions positively under a stable protocol. Second, it provides the mechanistic complement to the broader public robustness track: the public track tells us where the controller is still weak, while the bundled track tells us what the fully specified update controller can do when the benchmark makes all update types observable and causally well-labeled.
