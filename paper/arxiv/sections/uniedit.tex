\section{UniEdit: A Near-Ceiling Broad-Editing Sanity Check}

UniEdit broadens the benchmark surface beyond PopQA, FreshQA, and MQuAKE \citep{chen2025uniedit}. In our frozen official sample, however, it is almost perfectly solved by every official policy and therefore serves as a sanity check rather than a headline result.

Across FLAN-T5-Small, FLAN-T5-Base, Qwen2.5-1.5B-Instruct, and SmolLM2-360M-Instruct, retrieval-only is exactly 1.000 quality. The router and calibrated router are effectively tied at 0.999, and the no-\(V_{\text{future}}\) ablation ranges from 0.998 to 0.999. The benchmark-specific metrics tell the same story: edit success, generality, and locality are all between 0.9976 and 1.0000.

Because the benchmark is so close to saturation, some pairwise differences are statistically detectable but practically tiny. We therefore do not present UniEdit as evidence that the router wins. Instead, we use it as a broad-domain check that the boundary result from FreshQA and MQuAKE is not caused by a trivial implementation failure.
