We study a unified controller that routes language-model queries across six knowledge operations: parametric answering, memory read, retrieval, memory write, temporary adaptation, and guarded durable consolidation. The controller optimizes a single reward over quality, cost, forgetting risk, and delayed utility. On PopQA, the controller matches retrieval-level quality after conservative guardrails while reducing repeated retrieval on recurring queries. On a deterministic bundled freshness/update benchmark, it improves answer quality and stale-answer rate over retrieval-only and memory-only baselines while using meaningful temporary adaptation, rare durable consolidation, and rollback. These results support a narrow claim: routing knowledge operations jointly is more effective than treating retrieval, memory, and updates as isolated components.
