\section{PopQA: Recurring-Memory Routing}

The final PopQA result is intentionally narrow but clean. After a conservative non-recurring guardrail fix, the router matches retrieval-level quality overall and preserves recurring-case quality while reducing repeated retrieval on recurring cases. The key recurring statistics are:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.5em]
\item recurring quality remains at 0.181,
\item recurring retrieval falls from 1.000 for retrieval-only to 0.406,
\item recurring memory reads are substantial at 0.834,
\item recurring memory writes remain active at 0.398.
\end{itemize}

These ablations matter. The recurring-memory gain depends on \texttt{READ\_MEMORY}, \texttt{WRITE\_MEMORY}, and future-value-aware routing. The manual PopQA audit further shows that the pre-fix non-recurring failures were mostly avoidable routing mistakes, whereas the post-fix residual failures are mostly true retrieval/evidence misses.
