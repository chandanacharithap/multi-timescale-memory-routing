\section{UniEdit: A Near-Ceiling Sanity Check}

UniEdit broadens the benchmark surface beyond PopQA, FreshQA, and MQuAKE \citep{chen2025uniedit}. In our frozen official sample, however, it is almost perfectly solved by every official policy and therefore functions mainly as a sanity check.

Across all four official models, retrieval-only is exactly 1.000 quality. The router and calibrated router are effectively tied at 0.999, and \texttt{router\_no\_v\_future} ranges from 0.998 to 0.999. The benchmark-specific metrics tell the same story: edit success, generality, and locality are all between 0.9976 and 1.0000.

Because the benchmark is so close to saturation, some pairwise differences are statistically detectable but practically tiny. We therefore treat UniEdit as a broad-domain sanity check rather than as a source of strong positive or negative evidence about delayed utility.
